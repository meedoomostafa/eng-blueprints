# Calculus I: Course Index and Roadmap

This index serves as the master roadmap for organizing your notes for the Calculus I course, based on the lectures from the anaHr YouTube channel.

## Why Learn Calculus in This Order?

Calculus is a cumulative science. Each concept serves as a critical building block for the next:

1. **Properties and Types of Numbers**: The basic rules of our mathematical system. Understanding how real numbers behave and their completeness is necessary to understand limits.
    
2. **Functions**: The mathematical operations that process these numbers. We must understand their behavior, domains, and graphs before analyzing how they change.
    
3. **Limits**: The core tool of Calculus. This is the conceptual bridge that allows us to transition from finite mathematics to infinite mathematics.
    
4. **Continuity**: A direct application of limits. We must ensure functions are continuous (unbroken) to guarantee they can be safely differentiated or integrated.
    
5. **Derivatives**: The study of instantaneous rates of change. This is built upon finding the limit of a difference quotient as $\Delta x$ approaches zero.
    
6. **Integrals**: The accumulation of area under a curve. This inverse operation of differentiation is bound together by the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus.

### Topic 1: Properties and Types of Numbers

- **Description**:
    
    Before calculating rates of change or limits, we must understand the system we are working with: the Real Number System ($\mathbb{R}$). This topic establishes the core foundations of mathematics, covering Field Axioms (such as associativity: $(a \times b) \times c = a \times (b \times c)$), Order Axioms, and the concept of Completeness.
